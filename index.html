About the City

The Coolest Little Capital in the World

Wellington is the capital city of New Zealand, located at the southern tip of the North Island. Consistently voted one of the world's most liveable cities, it punches far above its weight in culture, cuisine, and creativity.

Nestled between a stunning natural harbour and steep bush-covered hills, Wellington is compact enough to explore on foot yet rich enough in experiences to keep you discovering for a lifetime. Its vibrant waterfront, world-renowned Te Papa museum, and thriving café culture make it a city unlike any other.

Home to the New Zealand Parliament, it is the political and cultural heart of Aotearoa — and with WETA Workshop nearby, a global centre for creative arts and film.

Climate

Wellington Weather

A temperate oceanic climate — prepare for all four seasons in one day!

☀️

Summer

Dec — Feb
18–23°C

Warm and occasionally hot. Long evenings, festivals, beach days at Oriental Bay. Can be breezy.

🍂

Autumn

Mar — May
12–18°C

Crisp and colourful — often the calmest season. Perfect for walking the harbour trails and hilltops.

❄️

Winter

Jun — Aug
6–12°C

Cool and wet with strong southerlies. Cosy café culture thrives. Occasional snow on the surrounding hills.

🌸

Spring

Sep — Nov
10–16°C

Variable and often windy. Cherry blossoms bloom in Newtown Park. Outdoor events return to the waterfront.

💨 Wellington is the windiest capital city in the world, with average wind speeds of 29 km/h year-round. Always pack a jacket — no matter the season!
Food, Arts & Culture

Life in Wellington

Wellington's creative soul spans food, film, music, theatre, and deep Māori heritage.

☕

Flat White Capital

Wellington claims to have invented the flat white. Dozens of world-class independent roasters compete fiercely for the perfect cup.

🍜

Diverse Dining

From ramen to fine dining, Wellington packs more dining diversity per capita than almost anywhere in Australasia.

🍺

Craft Beer Scene

Garage Project, Fork & Brewer, and dozens more pioneering breweries make Wellington New Zealand's undisputed beer capital.

🥗

Harbourside Market

The Sunday farmers' market at the waterfront is a local institution — fresh NZ produce, artisan goods, and incredible street food.

🎭

Theatre Scene

BATS, Circa, and the Opera House host world-class productions year-round. Wellington's intimate theatre scene is legendary across NZ.

🎵

Live Music

Jazz at Bodega, indie at San Fran, classical at the Michael Fowler Centre — Wellington's live music scene punches well above its weight.

🖼️

City Gallery

Civic Square's City Gallery presents cutting-edge contemporary art exhibitions from New Zealand and the world. Free entry.

🎪

Festivals

NZ International Arts Festival, Homegrown, LUX light festival, and the Wellington Jazz Festival fill the annual calendar.

🌊

Te Whanganui-a-Tara

Wellington's Māori name means "the great harbour of Tara." The region has deep roots with Ngāti Toa, Te Āti Awa, and Rangitāne iwi.

🏛️

Te Papa Museum

The Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa is a world-class institution celebrating Māori taonga and the nation's full story.

🎶

Kapa Haka

Traditional Māori performing arts — waiata, haka, and tītī torea — are celebrated across schools, marae, and public events city-wide.

📖

Te Reo Māori

Bilingual signage, public broadcasts in te reo, and language schools are woven into everyday life across the city.

🎬

WETA Workshop

Oscar-winning physical and digital effects studio behind The Lord of the Rings, Avatar, and hundreds of major international productions.

🧙

Middle-earth

Fans visit Weta Cave, Hobbiton, and iconic filming locations throughout the greater Wellington and Waikato regions.

💻

Tech Hub

Xero, TradeMe, and Flux Federation are among the growing list of leading tech companies headquartered in Wellington.

🎮

Games Industry

A thriving indie games development scene produces award-winning titles from studios tucked into Wellington's inner suburbs.

Getting Around

Transport in Wellington

Wellington is compact and walkable, with excellent public transport options.

🚌

Metlink Buses

An extensive bus network covers the CBD and suburbs. The Snapper card makes cashless travel easy across all routes.

🚆

Commuter Rail

Five rail lines connect Wellington to Hutt Valley, Porirua, Kapiti Coast, and Johnsonville. Fast and scenic commutes.

🚡

Cable Car

The iconic red cable car from Lambton Quay to Kelburn — a must-do for tourists and a beloved daily commute for locals since 1902.

🚴

Cycling

The Ara Harakeke cycleway and waterfront shared paths make cycling a great option around the flat CBD and harbour edge.

⛴️

Cook Strait Ferry

Interislander and Bluebridge ferries connect Wellington to Picton in the South Island — a stunning 3-hour journey through the Marlborough Sounds.

✈️

Airport (WLG)

Just 8km from the CBD with frequent domestic flights to Auckland, Christchurch, and Queenstown, plus international connections.

Timeline

A Brief History

From Polynesian settlement to creative capital — Wellington's remarkable story.

🌊
~1300 CE

Polynesian Settlement

Māori people settle the Wellington harbour region, naming it Te Whanganui-a-Tara — "the great harbour of Tara."

⚓
1839

European Settlement

The New Zealand Company establishes the first European settlement at Petone, later relocating to the sheltered harbour foreshore.

📜
1840

Treaty of Waitangi

New Zealand becomes a British colony. Wellington is formally established as a European town and named after the Duke of Wellington.

🏛️
1865

Becomes the Capital

The seat of government moves from Auckland to Wellington, recognising its central location between the North and South Islands.

🗳️
1893

Women's Suffrage

New Zealand becomes the first self-governing country to grant women the right to vote — a landmark law passed in Wellington's Parliament.

🚡
1902

Cable Car Opens

The iconic Wellington Cable Car begins service, connecting the CBD to Kelburn — still running today after over 120 years.

🎬
2001

Lord of the Rings

Peter Jackson's Fellowship of the Ring premieres globally, cementing Wellington's status as a world-class creative and film hub.

🌟
Today

Creative Capital

Wellington thrives as New Zealand's cultural, political, and creative heart — consistently ranked among the world's most liveable cities.
